Subjects Offered

Explore the military and civic subjects covered in the ROTC curriculum.

Basic ROTC Subjects


Military Organization

  • Introduces the structure and hierarchy of the Armed Forces of the Philippines.
  • Explains roles of different commands, units, and service branches.
  • Emphasizes unity, chain of command, and military teamwork.

Military Courtesy and Discipline

  • Teaches formal respect and ethical behavior in uniform.
  • Covers saluting, reporting procedures, and proper language use.
  • Instills a mindset of obedience and professionalism.

Military Customs and Traditions

  • Discusses time-honored practices like change of command and parades.
  • Strengthens esprit de corps and ROTC identity.
  • Promotes reverence for national symbols and ceremonies.

Threats to National Security

  • Identifies both internal and external security threats.
  • Explains terrorism, insurgency, cyberwarfare, and disasters.
  • Highlights the role of ROTC in national defense readiness.


Philippine Air Force Subjects


History of Aviation

  • Outlines key milestones in aviation, from Wright brothers to the PAF.
  • Connects early flight history to modern air defense capabilities.
  • Recognizes Filipino aviation pioneers and aircraft innovation.

Aircraft Recognition

  • Introduces visual characteristics of aircraft types.
  • Trains cadets in identifying aircraft silhouettes and purposes.
  • Links form, design, and mission type (e.g., fighter, transport, recon).

Air Defenses

  • Explains layered air defense systems and doctrine.
  • Discusses radar, missile defense, and air patrol operations.
  • Emphasizes the role of the Air Force in safeguarding Philippine airspace.


Search, Rescue & First Response


Basic Life Support (BLS)

  • Includes CPR, airway management, and rescue breathing.
  • Teaches proper assessment of unconscious victims and choking response.
  • Based on uploaded BLS module, aligned with emergency first responder standards.

First Aid

  • Covers wounds, fractures, burns, and bleeding control.
  • Focuses on life-saving response during emergencies before professionals arrive.
  • Hands-on skills such as bandaging, splinting, and casualty transport are taught.

Search and Rescue Operations

  • Involves locating and safely retrieving victims in emergencies or disaster zones.
  • Integrates teamwork, communication, and hazard mitigation.
  • Drills include urban, aquatic, and collapsed structure scenarios.


Drills, Ceremonies, and Events


Drills

  • Marching formations, facing movements, and rifle drills.
  • Emphasizes precision, timing, and group discipline.
  • Learned through synchronized practice during parades or inspections.

Military Ceremonies

  • Formal events such as change of command and flag retreats.
  • Cadets learn how to participate, host, and prepare protocol requirements.
  • Reinforces patriotism, tradition, and discipline.

Educational Tours

  • Field visits to military bases, aviation museums, or PAF camps.
  • Supports experiential learning of real-world military operations.
  • Boosts cadet morale and insight into military careers.

Environmental Awareness

  • Promotes ecological protection through tree planting and clean-up drives.
  • Highlights civic responsibility as part of ROTC values.

Gift Giving / Outreach

  • Cadets organize charity events for communities or local shelters.
  • Develops compassion, volunteerism, and people-oriented leadership.

ROTC Games & Competitions

  • Events like rifle assembly races, obstacle courses, and knowledge quizzes.
  • Foster friendly rivalry and showcase military proficiency.

Team Building Activities

  • Group exercises that build camaraderie, communication, and trust.
  • Includes outdoor challenges and leadership simulations.
